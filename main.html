My Favorite Holiday Destination: Purulia, West Bengal


Purulia is a picturesque district located in the state of West Bengal, known for its scenic beauty, rich culture, and vibrant traditions. Nestled in the Chota Nagpur Plateau, it is characterized by rolling hills, lush forests, and a variety of wildlife.

  1. Rakab Forest - Keshargarh is famous for Rakab Forest which was known to be the Forest of 16 cross.
  2. Muradi Dam - About 15 km from Panchyet Hill.
  3. Charidah - Masks of Charidah village in Baghmundi block of Purulia (WB) used in the martial dance “Chhau”.
  4. Sita Kundu - Located in Bagandi village under Ajodhya Gram Panchayat at Baghmundi Block of Purulia district.
  5. Turga Falls - A cascading waterfall with very clear water.
  • Bengali Curries - Huge variety of great Bengali curry recipes.
  • Tandoori Specials - Dishes cooked in a traditional clay oven.
  • Biryanis - A mixed rice dish featuring meat or seafood and aromatic spices.
  • Pani Pur - A deep-fried, hollow spherical shell filled with flavored water.
  • Pav Bhaji - Spicy mashed veggies served with dinner rolls topped with butter.
